Productivity isn't about working more hours; it's about making every hour count. Here are 10 tips to optimize your workflow.

## 1. Master Your IDE

Stop using the mouse. Learn the keyboard shortcuts for your favorite IDE (VS Code, WebStorm).
*   **Cmd/Ctrl + P**: Quick Open File
*   **Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + F**: Global Search
*   **Cmd/Ctrl + D**: Multi-cursor selection

## 2. The Pomodoro Technique

Work for 25 minutes, break for 5. This keeps your brain fresh and prevents burnout.

## 3. Automate Repetitive Tasks

If you do something more than twice, write a script for it.
*   Use `npm` scripts for build pipelines.
*   Use shell aliases for common git commands (`gco` instead of `git checkout`).

## 4. Local-First Tooling

Use tools like **Utildex** to perform quick tasks (JSON formatting, UUID generation) without context switching to a slow web page full of ads.

## 5. Clear Your Context

Close tabs you aren't using. A cluttered browser leads to a cluttered mind.

## 6. Write Documentation for Yourself

When you solve a hard bug, write down how you did it. You *will* encounter it again in 6 months.

## 7. Code Reviews are Learning Opportunities

Don't just look for bugs. Look for patterns, style, and architectural decisions.

## 8. Sleep

You cannot debug complex race conditions on 4 hours of sleep. Rest is part of the job.

## 9. Block Distractions

Turn off Slack notifications when you are in "Deep Work" mode.

## 10. Continuous Learning

Spend 30 minutes a day learning something new. It compounds over time.
